Motor Driver — L298N (Deep Dive)

The L298N is a popular dual H-bridge motor driver module. It can drive two DC motors independently, handle direction control and accept PWM for speed control.

Why we use it

- Supplies higher current to motors that Arduino cannot provide directly.
- Allows reversing motor polarity (forward/reverse).
- Takes control signals (IN1/IN2/IN3/IN4) and PWM enable signals.

Common pins (on typical module):
  • VCC (motor power) — connect battery (e.g., 7.4V)
  • 5V EN (sometimes jumper) — powers logic (if regulator present)
  • GND — common ground with Arduino
  • IN1 / IN2 — control motor A direction
  • IN3 / IN4 — control motor B direction
  • ENA / ENB — PWM pins to control speed
  • OUT1..OUT4 — outputs to motors

Practical tips

- Check the stall current of your motor. L298N can handle ~2A per channel on some modules (but check specs).
- Use adequate battery: low voltage causes weak torque, too high may damage motor/driver.
- Add decoupling capacitors and common ground for stable signals.
