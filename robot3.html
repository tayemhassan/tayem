DC Motors — What & How

In our car we use DC geared motors. These are small DC motors with a gearbox attached to:

  • Increase torque (push the car)
  • Reduce speed to a usable RPM for precise control

Important motor specs

Voltage: choose motors that match battery (e.g., 6V or 12V)

Stall current: the max current at start — must be supported by motor driver

RPM & gear ratio: decide speed vs torque

How we control them

Arduino cannot drive motors directly. Instead:

  • Arduino outputs direction signals (HIGH/LOW) for each motor input.
  • Arduino outputs PWM to control speed (analogWrite).
  • The motor driver supplies the current from the battery to the motors.
